Alan Watts

Brief history

Alan Wilson Watts (6 January 1915 16 November 1973) was an English writer, speaker and self-styled "philosophical entertainer",[2] known for interpreting and popularising Japanese, Chinese and Indian traditions of Buddhist, Taoist, and Hindu philosophy for a Western audience. Watts was born to middle-class parents in the village of Chislehurst, Ken. Watts spent several holidays in France in his teen years, It was not long afterward that Watts felt forced to decide between the Anglican Christianity he had been exposed to and the Buddhism he had read about in various libraries, including Croshaw's. and chose , Buddhism.. in 1938 Alan became an Episcopal, after moving to the states. Then relocated to Millbrock, New york where he wrote his book he Wisdom of Insecurity: A Message for an Age of Anxiety. in 1951 he began teaching Buddist studies in San Francisco. Then in 1956 he started a radio show called “Way Beyond the West.” His radio show broadcasted nationally. Up until his passing in 1973, he continued to write and travel.
